Saving the Environment

RECYCLING WASTE

“At its core, the issue of a clean environment is a matter of public health.” Reduce, reuse, and recycle. Cut down on what you throw away. Follow the three "R's" to conserve natural resources and landfill space.

AIR QUALITY

“Environmental pollution is not only humanity’s treason to humanity but also a treason to all other living creatures on earth!” Don't burn wood or trash. Burning firewood and trash are among the major sources of particle pollution (soot) in many parts of the country.

CLEAN WATER

Conserve water. The less water you use, the less runoff and wastewater that eventually end up in the ocean.Don't send chemicals into our waterways. Choose non-toxic chemicals in the home and office.

WILDLIFE

Habitat destruction is the main threat to 85 percent of all threatened and endangered species, according to the International Union for Conservation of Nature. You can help reduce this threat by planting native trees, restoring wetlands or cleaning up beaches in your area.

Plantation

Compost your leaf waste to avoid use of chemical fertilisers. Do not present flower bouquets. Instead, give a potted plant and encourage your friends to grow plants. Start a tree planting programme in your area.

ECOSYSTEM

As the earth's human population has increased, natural ecosystems have declined and changes in the balance of natural cycles has had a negative impact on both humans and other living systems.One powerful way to minimize the environmental impact of driving is to trade in your clunker for a more eco-friendly vehicle. For other (less costly) options, try these tips to get you where you need to go.
